Program Overview

The Co-op Program is AUI's flagship work-based learning initiative, designed to bridge the gap between academic theory and professional practice. Students work full-time with our industry partners while maintaining their academic progression.

This program provides students with the opportunity to apply classroom knowledge in real-world settings, develop professional skills, and build networks that will benefit their future careers. Our partner companies span various industries including technology, finance, engineering, and consulting.

Eligibility Requirements

  • ✓
    Academic Standing: Minimum cumulative GPA of 3.0 and good academic standing with no disciplinary issues.
  • ✓
    Course Completion: Completion of foundational courses in your major and at least 60 credit hours.
  • ✓
    Faculty Recommendation: Letter of recommendation from a faculty member in your department.
  • ✓
    Professional Skills: Demonstrated communication skills, professionalism, and relevant technical competencies.
  • ✓
    Time Commitment: Availability for full-time work (40 hours/week) for the program duration.

Application Process & Timeline

  • Application Submission

    Complete online application including essays, resume, and academic transcripts. Applications open each semester.

  • Initial Review

    Academic and professional background review by the WIL team. Candidates may be invited for an interview.

  • Company Matching

    Successful applicants are matched with partner companies based on interests, skills, and availability.

  • Company Interview

    Final interview with the host company to ensure mutual fit and discuss project expectations.

  • Placement Confirmation

    Formal placement confirmation and orientation session before starting the co-op experience.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is the Co-op program different from a regular internship? +
The Co-op program is more structured and longer than typical internships. It includes academic credit, faculty supervision, regular check-ins, and a formal learning portfolio. Students also receive comprehensive career support throughout the experience.
Are Co-op positions paid? +
Most Co-op positions are paid, with salaries varying by industry and company. Some positions may offer other benefits like housing stipends, transportation, or professional development allowances.
Can international students participate? +
Yes, international students can participate. However, they may need to obtain appropriate work authorization. Our team works with international students to navigate visa and work permit requirements.
